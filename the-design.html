THE DESIGN

How social media apps are designed to keep you addicted.

THE FEATURES

  • Ephemeral Content

    content that is only available for a limited time and then vanishes. It feeds into the FOMO (fear of missing out) culture.

  • Infinite Scroll

    Feeding into endless content can put you in a trance, innovation kept users looking at their phones for longer than necessary. It's essentially an in-pocket slot machine.

  • Advertisements

    Personalized for the user. Creepy enough, your devices listen to you while you speak, detect keywords and personalize your ads according to them.

  • Personalized Algorithm

    Uses your data to keep you wrapped up in the content you consume. Personalized feeds have progressed to recognizing the things you prefer to see before interacting with them.

  • Notifications

    Built a routine of checking our phone and apps when there's nothing to see. Falsely hearing our phone vibrate when nothing actually happened.

  • Clickbait Thumbnails

    TikTok itself promotes extreme and oversexualized thumbnails - an app that’s mainly used by teens. 32.5% of TikTok users are aged between 10 and 19. The age restriction is 12+, but there are 10 year olds using this app?

  • Like Button

    Posting an image or tweet that gains a lot of likes ultimately translate into three of the most powerful reinforcers — attention, approval, and affection from other people. You end up posting more, each time trying to out-perform your previous posts like count.

  • Anonymity

    It can be a good thing for some, but the majority of the time, anonymous people are provoking arguments, spreading hate, violence, etc online. This prevents social media companies from being able to hold these individuals accountable.

BEHAVIOUR

  • Harmful Trends/Content

    Being exposed to harmful or oversexualized/graphic content can rewire the developing brain Oversexualized dances performed by minors (ex. WAP dance, hip-thrusting, twerking, sexual dance challenges). In fact, there are many users that normalize grooming and romaticize the idea of dating older men.

  • Comparison

    Trends that only benefit those that have eurocentric features and that have won the biological lottery: hourglass waist trend, symmetrical face trend, nice nose profile trend, baggy clothes vs bikini trend, “glow up” trend (for those who are attractive and align with eurocentric beauty standards).

  • Misogynistic Trends

    Trends about waist size, nose profile, face symmetry, light eyes, light skin, thick hair, etc. are more popular than promoting body neutrality. Many users participating in these trends end up more anxious and depressed about their looks.

  • Skipping Pre-Teen Years

    Because of the cringe culture, children are growing up faster than they should. They don’t go through their awkward phase, they don’t transition from childhood to preteen years, they’re catapulted into their teen years, having to present themselves older than they are.

  • Living Through our Phones

    Instead of talking to friends friends or family, social media seems more entertaining. We watch other people live their lives through social media, and when we get to do the same, we go on our phones. Real-life doesn’t provide us with the same serotonin boost as social media does.

  • Popularity > Privacy

    What you post on the internet stays forever. Many TikTok users casually share too much information and explicit content to their followers. It ranges from specifics in their intimate life, people they despise, to hateful content — all for views and validation.

Looking at it as a whole, you may think, “It’s so obvious that this is a problem!” but it isn’t. These app were designed to make you addicted, implement features to seem like they care about their users, but just enough so that their in control of your addiction.
